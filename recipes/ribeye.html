Ribeye steak

Description

Even a bad ribeye steak is good! It is easy to impress and delight your guests with this dish for a weekend dinner. Remember to pair this with a nice red wine!

Ingredients

  • 1x large Ribeye steak (approx. 350g) about 3cm thick
  • 1x tbsp grape seed Oil (or other with high smoke point)
  • 4x tbsp salted butter
  • 5x garlic cloves
  • A few thyme or rosemary sprigs
  • Flaky sea salt and black pepper to taste

Steps

  1. Remove the ribeye steak an hour prior to cooking, allowing it to reach room temperature naturally
  2. Pat dry the ribeye with paper towels. This is a secret for a nice crust!
  3. Set your frying pan (preferably cast iron) to high heat.
  4. Add a generous amount of flaky sea salt over both sides of the steak. Repeat with black pepper to taste.
  5. Add the grape seed oil to the pan and let it heat up. Add the steak just before it starts smoking.
  6. Add the garlic cloves and the thyme/rosemary sprigs.
  7. Fry the steak for about 3 minutes on each side. Don't move it around too much to allow for a nice crust to form.
  8. Add the butter to the pan.
  9. Fry an additional 2 minutes on each side of the steak, basting it in the melted butter.
  10. Remove the steak from the pan and let it rest for 5 minutes before slicing. This will help finish the cooking and to keep the meat juices sealed in the meat.
  11. While the steak is resting, add a small amount of water to the pan and let it boil. Scrape the brown bits from the pan and let it reduce for a couple of minutes.
  12. Remove the thyme/rosemary sprigs and discard. Drizzle the juices from the pan along with the garlic over the steak. You can also add this to a saucer and serve at the table.
  13. Enjoy while it is still warm and fresh!
